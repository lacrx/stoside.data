Staff submits the ﬁrst quarter Financial Status Report and recommends that the City

BACKGROUND
The City Council adopted the FY 2021-22 budgets on June 2, 2021. with approved
General Fund revenues of $171.17 million and expenditures of $170.14 million. The City
adopted budget for all funds is $547.9 million in revenues and $546.79 million in
expenditures.
The report summarizes the preliminary General Fund major operating funds results as
of September 30, 2021, and is not intended to be inclusive of all transactions.
It
compares budget and actual for revenues and expenditures. The financial status of the
Water Utilities and Harbor enterprise funds are also included. The report is intended
only to provide the City Council and the public with a preliminary overview of the state of
the City's general ﬁscal condition as of September 30. 2021.
As of September 30, 2021. total General Fund revenues came in at $20 million and
represent 12 percent of total revenue budget. The revenue totals are 9 percent higher
than the ﬁrst quarter of FY 2020-21. Total General Fund expenditures for the first
quarter amount to $38.26 million and represent 22% of the total expenditure budget.

Property Taxes represent 44 percent of the total General Fund revenues and are primarily received in the
DeoemberIJanuary and AprilIMay time frames. Property tax revenues through September 30 are at 2 percent
of the amended budget. Compared to the prior year. property tax revenue decreased 29 percent. The large
decrease is due to timing of payments: the second apportionment was posted in the first quarter of the prior
year but won’t be posted until second quarter of the current year. Continued building development and
increasing housing prices are expected to continue throughout FY2021-22.
Sales Taxes represent 14 percent of the total annual General Fund revenues. Revenues received through
September 30 are at 12 percent of the adopted budget and are 20 percent higher compared to the prior year.
Strong internet sales. general consumer goods sales, and building and construction supplies sales due to the
booming home improvement market continued from the fourth quarter of last year.
Transient Occupancy Tax (TOT) represent 4 percent of total General Fund revenues. The City collects 10
percent of the room rent charged by a hotel or vacation rental operator to tourists occupying a room within the
City for a period of less than 30 days. The City also collects a 1.5 percent assessment of the room rent which
is passed through to Visit Oceanside for marketing purposes. As of September 30, TOT is at 22 percent of
adopted budget. TOT is up 141 percent compared to prior year actuals due to decreased travel and tourism in
the prior year as a result of the COVlD-19 pandemic.

ENTERPRISE FUNDS
Enterprise funds, also known as business-type activities, are used to account for the acquisition, operation and
maintenance of governmental facilities and services that are entirely or predominantly self-supporting through
user charges. The operations of Enterprise Funds are accounted for in such a manner as to show a proﬁt or
loss similar to comparable private enterprise. The ﬁnancial status of operating funds for Water, Sewer and
Solid Waste Disposal are discussed below.
Please note that Water and Sewer budgets consist of multiple
funds (operating, replacement and developer connection).
Debt service is not included. Generally accepted
accounting principles (GAAP) call for ﬁscal analysis of Enterprise Funds to reﬂect a roll up of all funds into one
report.
WATER FUND REVENUES
Overall revenues for the Combined Water Funds are at 26 percent of the amended budget as of September 30
as shown in Table 4.
Water accounts receivables have increased during COVIDu19. While the majority of customers are paying their
bills in full each month. there are businesses as well as residential customers that are struggling to pay their
bills. Staff is reaching out to the delinquent customers offering reasonable payment plans to help them become
current. In an effort to help delinquent customers, the City is currently offering an Oceanside Ratepayer Relief
Program of up to a $500 credit to assist Oceanside customers that are behind on their utility bill. The program
is available on a ﬁrst-come, ﬁrst-served basis to all single—family residential, multi-family, commercial, irrigation,
agriculture and recycled water customers that meet the program eligibility criteria and requirements.
