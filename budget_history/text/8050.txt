The 
annual 
operating 
budget 
document  serves as the City’s 
monetary statement of program and 
service delivery for the next twelve 
months.  This brochure has been 
developed to show a snapshot of the 
general fund budget. 
 
City of Oceanside revenue continues 
to show encouraging signs of growth.  
Recent market data indicates that 
housing sales and prices continue to 
increase with retail sales remaining 
relatively flat.  As tourism takes off in 
the City, so is increased transient 
occupancy 
tax 
(TOT) 
revenue.  
Developer-related 
fees 
also 
are 
remaining solid. 
 
However, along with the increase in 
revenues, there is also an increase in 
retirement cost.  Recent changes by 
CalPERS in actuarial assumptions 
and rate smoothing methods has 
resulted in significant rate increases 
over the next several years.  The City 
is being proactive regarding this 
increase in retirement cost by setting 
aside $1.0 million in this budget to 
offset the effects of this increase.  This 
issue 
will 
continue 
to 
provide 
challenges to the City in its effort to 
provide services to the community.
