SYNOPSIS
Staff submits the third quarter Financial Status Report and recommends that the City
Council accept the report and approve the budget adjustments.
BACKGROUND
The City Council adopted the FY 2022-23 budgets on June 8, 2022, with approved
General Fund revenues of $188.91 million and expenditures of $186.85 million. The
City’s adopted budget for all funds is $569.79 million in revenues and $592.72 million in
expenditures.
The report summarizes the preliminary General Fund major operating funds results as
of March 31, 2023, and is not intended to be inclusive of all transactions. It compares
budget and actual for revenues and expenditures. The financial status of the Water
Utilities and Harbor enterprise funds are also included. The report is intended only to
provide the City Council and the public with a preliminary overview of the state of the
City’s general fiscal condition as of March 31 , 2023.
As of March 31, 2023, total General Fund revenues came in at $132.7 million and
represent 70 percent of total revenue budget of $188.9 million. The revenue totals are 9
percent higher than the third quarter of FY 2021-22. Total General Fund expenditures
for the third quarter amount to $141.5 million and represent 72 percent of the total
expenditure budget.

ENTERPRISE FUNDS
Enterprise funds, also known as business-type activities, are used to account for the acquisition, operation 
and
maintenance of governmental facilities and services that are entirely or predominantly 
self-supporting through user
charges. The operations of Enterprise Funds are accounted for in such a manner as to show a profit or loss similar to
comparable private enterprise. The financial status of operating funds for Water, Sewer and Solid Waste Disposal are
discussed below. 
Please note that Water and Sewer budgets consist of multiple 
funds (operating, replacement and
developer connection). 
Debt service is not included. Generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP) call for fiscal
analysis of Enterprise Funds to reflect a roll up of all funds into one report.
WATER FUND REVENUES
Overall revenues for the Combined Water Funds are at 75 percent of the amended budget as of March 31 as shown in
Table 4.
Water accounts receivables have increased during COVID-19. While the majority of customers are paying their bills in full
each month, there are businesses as well as residential customers that are struggling to pay their bills. Staff is reaching
out to the delinquent customers offering reasonable payment plans to help them become current.
WATER FUND EXPENDITURES
Table 4 highlights the amount and percentage expended by each water program. As of March 31, the Water Combined
Funds expended 67 percent of the amended operating budget. 
Operating revenues exceed operating expenditures
including transfers and depreciation by $31.5 million. This is mostly due to the transfer of bond proceeds from the Water
Debt Service Fund to the Capital Project Fund. As of March 31, capital project expenses and encumbered by contract are
at 37 percent of the total budget. Expenditures for capital projects can vary depending on the stage of each construction
project.
