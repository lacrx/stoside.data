EXECUTIVE SUMMARY 
 
The annual operating budget document serves 
as the City’s monetary statement of program and 
service delivery for the next 12 months.  This 
brochure has been developed to show a 
snapshot of the general fund budget. 
The revenue projections in the Five-Year 
Forecast were conservatively estimated based 
on the best available information and uncertainty 
about the COVID-19 pandemic’s impact on 
revenues as of January 2021.  With three 
additional months of financial data available, it is 
clear that the City is weathering the pandemic in 
a stronger financial position than anticipated and 
sales tax revenue continues to be strong.  
The adopted general fund budget assumes that 
the current budget forms the base for 
Maintenance & Operations (M&O), including 
restoration of the 10% M&O budget cuts 
instituted due to COVID.   
Compared to the pre-COVID FY 2019-20 
Adopted Budget, expenditures are increased by 
$12.79M including $11.23M in personnel 
expenses and $1.56M in M&O.   The cost 
increases are offset by $13M in revenue 
increases, primarily from property taxes and 
increased sales taxes as detailed in the Five-
Year Forecast.   
The FY 2021-22 General Fund budget will not 
use any one-time money to cover expenditures 
but will be funded through actual revenues.  As 
always, expenditures will be watched very 
closely to ensure that departments do not 
exceed their budget allotment and to capitalize 
on any potential savings.  Importantly, 
departments are continually reviewing 
efficiencies and evaluating service deliveries.   
The adopted budget is fiscally conservative; as 
forecasts become more certain as the economy 
recovers, further restorations can be considered 
to enhance service to the community.
