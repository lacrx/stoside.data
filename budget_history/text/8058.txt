The annual operating budget document  
serves as the City’s monetary statement 
of program and service delivery for the 
next twelve months.  This brochure has 
been developed to show a snapshot of 
the general fund budget. 
 
The City of Oceanside has a balanced 
budget; revenue continues to show 
signs of growth.  Recent market data 
indicates that housing sales and values 
continue to improve with retail sales 
remaining relatively flat.  As tourism 
becomes a more important economic 
driver, transient occupancy tax (TOT) 
revenue is on the rise.  Developer-
related fees also are remaining solid. 
 
The General Fund (GF) budget for FY 
2019-20 
includes 
a 
“rollover” 
of 
Maintenance & Operations (M&O) from 
the current year; however, a modest 
CPI of $362,738 was included in the 
forecasted 
expenditure 
budget 
of 
$157.62 million to better reflect known 
obligations 
such 
as 
multiple 
year 
contracts.  In Personnel Services, the 
proposed 
expenditure 
allocation 
includes all known and negotiated 
personnel and benefit costs plus a 
placeholder for the labor associations 
that will be negotiated in FY 2019-20.  
 
In November 2018, Oceanside voters 
approved Measure X, a temporary 
(seven-year) 
one-half 
percent 
local 
sales and use tax for seven years to 
provide funding to maintain and improve 
general city services. 
 
In 
addition 
to 
the 
General 
Fund 
operating budget of $157.62M, $13.89M 
has been budgeted for Measure X 
funded projects.
