BACKGROUND
The City Council adopted the FY 2020-21 budgets on June 3, 2020, with approved
General Fund revenues of $158.03 million and expenditures of $157.6 million. The City
adopted budget for all funds is $485.58 million in revenues and $533.27 million in
expenditures.
The report summarizes the preliminary General Fund major operating funds results as of
March 31, 2021 , and is not intended to be inclusive of all transactions. It compares budget
and actual for revenues and expenditures. The financial status of the Water Utilities and
Harbor enterprise funds are also included. The report is intended only to provide Council
and the public with an overview of the state of the City's general fiscal condition as of
March 31, 2021.
Due to the COVID-19 Pandemic and resulting economic impacts, the FY 2020-21 General
Fund budget was reduced from the 2020 Five-Year Financial Forecast. Property tax was
reduced $2.0 million, sales tax was reduced $2.4 million, transient occupancy tax (TOT)
was reduced $3.0 million. To balance the budget, proposed expenditures were reduced
$4.29 million for vacant positions, maintenance and operations was reduced $2.1 million
(10 percent reduction), and internal service funds were reduced $1.34 million.
As of March 31, 2021, total revenues are at $108 million and are 4 percent higher than
the third quarter of FY 2019-20. Due to the cyclical nature of some revenue sources,
revenues do not flow evenly by quarter, however Oceanside’s revenues are strong and
outperforming expectations.

Property Taxes represent 43 percent of the total General Fund revenues and are primarily received in the
December/January and April/May time frames. Property tax revenues through March 31 are at 62 percent of
the adopted budget. Compared to the prior year, property tax revenue increased 11 percent. The property tax
revenue increase is consistent with the projected increase in overall assessed values of properties in
Oceanside. Continued building development and increasing housing prices are expected to continue throughout
FY 2020-21.
Sales Taxes represent 13.5 percent of the total annual General Fund revenues. Revenues received through
March 31 are at 72 percent of the adopted budget and are up 5 percent compared to the prior year actual of
$14.7M. Casual restaurants continue to suffer due to limited service as well as fuel and service stations.
Otfsetting the decline are significant increases in online sales, home improvement. food, and discount
department stores sales.
Transient Occupancy Tax represent 3.7 percent of total General Fund revenues. The City collects 10 percent
of the room rent charged by a hotel or vacation rental operator to tourists occupying a room within the City for
a period of less than 30 days. The City also collects a 1.5 percent assessment of the room rent which is passed
through to Visit Oceanside for marketing purposes. As of March 31, TOT is at 82 percent of adopted budget.
TOT is down 10 percent compared to prior year actuals of $5.4M. COVlD-19 continues to impact revenues for
hotellmotels and short-term rentals.

Enterprise funds, also known as business-type activities. are used to account for the acquisition, operation and
maintenance of governmental facilities and services that are entirely or predominantly self-supporting through
user charges. The operations of Enterprise Funds are accounted for in such a manner as to show a profit or
loss similar to comparable private enterprise.

Solid Waste Disposal are discussed below.
Please note that Water and Sewer budgets consist of multiple
funds (operating, replacement and developer connection). Debt service is not included. Generally accepted
accounting principles (GAAP) call for fiscal analysis of Enterprise Funds to reflect a roll up of all funds into one
report.
