BACKGROUND
The City Council adopted the FY 2020-21 budgets on June 3, 2020, with approved
General Fund revenues of $158.03 million and expenditures of $157.6 million. The City
adopted budget for all funds is $485.58 million in revenues and $533.27 million in
expenditures.
The report summarizes the preliminary General Fund major operating funds results as of
June 30, 2021. and is not intended to be inclusive of all transactions. It compares budget
and actual for revenues and expenditures. The financial status of the Water Utilities and
Harbor enterprise funds are also included. The report is intended only to provide Council
and the public with an overview of the state of the City's general fiscal condition as of
June 30, 2021.
Due to the COVlD-19 Pandemic and resulting economic impacts, the FY 2020-21 General
Fund budget was reduced from the 2020 Five-Year Financial Forecast. Property tax was
reduced $2.0 million. sales tax was reduced $2.4 million, transient occupancy tax (TOT)
was reduced $3.0 million. To balance the budget, proposed expenditures were reduced
$4.29 million for vacant positions. maintenance and operations was reduced $2.1 million
(10 percent reduction), and internal service funds were reduced $1.34 million.
The FY 2020-21 ended with higher than expected property tax, sales tax, and transient
occupancy tax revenues. The preliminary results for the year ending June 30, 2021 show
that the General Fund will have a $19.4 million surplus. Preliminary total revenues are at
$108.0 million and are 4.0 percent higher than the prior year. Total General Fund
expenditures for the year ending June 30, 2021 amount to $155.99 million. 5 percent
below the prior year. Departments have consistently controlled expenses throughout the

GENERAL FUND BUDGET POSITION
The City Council adopted the FY 2020-21 budgets on June 3, 2020, with approved General Fund revenues of
$158.03 million and expenditures of $157.6 million.
As a result of the COVlD-19 pandemic and resulting
economic changes, the February 2020 Five-Year Financial Forecast required an $8.89M reduction. The
changes are reflected in the adopted budget. Property tax was reduced $2.0 million. sales tax was reduced
$2.4 million. transient occupancy tax (TOT) was reduced $3.0 million. To balance the budget, proposed
expenditures were reduced $4.29 million for vacant positions. maintenance and operations was reduced $2.1
million (10 percent reduction). and internal service funds were reduced $1.34 million.
Throughout the report, amounts in the tables listed as Amended Budget include supplemental appropriations
approved by Council throughout the year.
GENERAL FUND PRELIMINARY RESULTS
The COVlD-19 pandemic impact on revenues were minimal. The General Fund is estimated to end the year
with a surplus of $19.4 million. Contributing to the surplus were property taxes. sales and use taxes and
occupancy taxes in total exceeded budget by $13.2 million. General Fund expenditures were $12.0 million
lower than budgeted. CARES funds and other COVlD-19 reimbursements of $5.2 million contributed to the
lower expenditures. Table 1 and Table 2 provide a more detailed picture of year-end results.

Property Taxes represent 43 percent of the total General Fund revenues and are primarily received in the
December/January and AprilIMay time frames. Properly tax revenues through June 30 are at 106 percent of
the adopted budget. Compared to the prior year, property tax revenue increased 6 percent. The property tax
revenue increase is consistent with the projected increase in overall assessed values of properties in
Oceanside. Continued building development and increasing housing prices are expected to continue into FY
2021-22.
Sales Taxes represent 13.5 percent of the total annual General Fund revenues. Revenues received through
June 30 are at 127 percent of the adopted budget and are up 13 percent compared to the prior year actual of
$23.8M. The largest factor in this improvement is the allocation from the San Diego County use-tax pool. This
allocation resulted in a $437,000 addition or 58% increase from 2019 in the allocation that we receive. Additional
growth was seen in general consumer goods with family apparel and sporting goods stores up $166,000 or
12% compared to 2019. Building and construction continued strong with an increase of $111,000 or 20% over
2019.
Transient Occupancy Tax budget represents 3.7 percent of total General Fund revenues. The City collects 10
percent of the room rent charged by a hotel or vacation rental operator to tourists occupying a room within the
City for a period of less than 30 days. The City also collects a 1.5 percent assessment of the room rent which
is passed through to Visit Oceanside for marketing purposes. As of June 30. TOT is at 157 percent of adopted
budget. The total includes approximately $160,000 from the new Beach Resort Hotels. This revenue is subject
to a rebate agreement and a corresponding amount is shown as an expense in the General Fund non-
departmental account. Excluding these revenues, TOT is up 22.8 percent from the prior year.

ENTERPRISE FUNDS
Enterprise funds, also known as business-type activities. are used to account for the acquisition, operation and
maintenance of governmental facilities and services that are entirely or predominantly self-supporting through
user charges. The operations of Enterprise Funds are accounted for in such a manner as to show a profit or
loss similar to comparable private enterprise.
The financial status of operating funds for Water, Sewer and
Solid Waste Disposal are discussed below.
Please note that Water and Sewer budgets consist of multiple
funds (operating, replacement and developer connection). Debt service is not included. Generally accepted
accounting principles (GAAP) call for fiscal analysis of Enterprise Funds to reflect a roll up of all funds into one
report.
WATER FUND REVENUES
Overall revenues for the Combined Water Funds are at 122 percent of the amended budget as of June 30 as
shown in Table 4.
Water accounts receivables have increased during COVlD-19. While the majority of customers are paying their
bills in full each month. there are business as well as residential customers that are struggling to pay their bills.
A new grant program to help delinquent customers was rolled out this summer. Staff is also reaching out to the
delinquent customers offering reasonable payment plans.
