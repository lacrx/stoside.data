EXECUTIVE SUMMARY 
 
The annual operating budget document serves as 
the City’s monetary statement of program and 
service delivery for the next 12 months. This 
document provides a snapshot of the General 
Fund budget.  The City’s General Fund, 
comprising approximately 41 percent of the total 
operating appropriations, supports the majority of 
City services.   
The General Fund revenues, projected at 
$216.28M were conservatively estimated based 
on best available information.  Property, Sales, 
and Transient Occupancy Taxes are the primary 
sources of the City’s General Fund revenues.  
The General Fund expenditures budget is 
$218.71M; $215.28M are ongoing expenditures 
and $3.43M are one-time costs from reserves. 
Therefore, it is a structurally balanced budget. 
The 
current 
economic 
environment 
was 
considered in developing the budget. To address 
inflation, a 3.5% cost of living increase to the M&O 
budget is included. Known contract price 
increases and reasonable placeholders for future 
bargaining unit agreements were also included to 
help the City respond to the competitive labor 
market and wage growth seen nationwide. If 
necessary, adjustments will be made to cut back 
on expenses and/or explore means to raise 
additional revenue. 
Efficiently 
managing 
the 
City's 
increasing 
CalPERS costs remains a top priority. The City 
has been taking proactive measures to mitigate 
the impact its rising cost. 
The FY2024-25 operating budget continues to 
focus on the Council’s collective priorities 
including maintaining the Quality of Life for 
residents, Public Safety, Economic Development 
and addressing Homelessness.  It reflects our 
shared vision for the City of Oceanside—a thriving 
community that embraces fiscal responsibility, 
prioritizes community well-being, and champions 
environmental stewardship. It is a blueprint for 
progress, ensuring that we meet the challenges of 
today while building a resilient and prosperous 
future.
