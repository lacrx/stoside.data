The annual operating budget document  
serves as the City’s monetary statement 
of program and service delivery for the 
next twelve months.  This brochure has 
been developed to show a snapshot of the 
general fund budget. 
 
The City of Oceanside has a balanced 
budget; revenue continues to show signs 
of growth.  Recent market data indicates 
that housing sales and values continue to 
improve 
with 
retail 
sales 
remaining 
relatively flat.  As tourism becomes a 
more important economic driver, transient 
occupancy tax (TOT) revenue is on the 
rise.  Developer-related fees also are 
remaining solid. 
 
The General Fund (GF) budget for FY 
2018-19 
includes 
a 
“rollover” 
of 
Maintenance & Operations (M&O) from 
the current year; however, a modest CPI 
of 
$372,240 
was 
included 
in 
the 
forecasted expenditure budget of $151.74 
million to better reflect known obligations 
such as multiple year contracts.  In 
Personnel 
Services, 
the 
proposed 
expenditure allocation includes all known 
and negotiated personnel and benefit 
costs plus a placeholder for the labor 
associations that will be in negotiations in 
FY 2018-19.
