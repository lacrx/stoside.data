EXECUTIVE SUMMARY 
 
The annual operating budget document serves 
as the City’s monetary statement of program and 
service delivery for the next 12 months.  This 
brochure shows a snapshot of the General Fund 
budget. 
The City’s General Fund, approximately 36 
percent of the total operating appropriations, 
supports the majority of City services.  
Revenues, projected at $204.02M were 
conservatively estimated based on best available 
information.  Revenue growth is primarily 
attributed to strong property tax, sales tax and 
transient occupancy tax (TOT) revenue growth. 
Revenue forecasts provided by an industry 
expert -HdL Companies were utilized to forecast 
property and sales tax revenues. TOT revenues 
were forecasted based on historical data and 
timing of hotel development completion. 
The General Fund expenditures total $203.29M.  
This includes a transfer of $0.95M to Capital 
Improvement Program.  The current economic 
environment was considered in developing the 
budget. To address inflation, staff added a 3.5 
percent cost of living increase to maintenance 
and operations budget. Known bargaining 
agreement costs and reasonable placeholders 
for increases to future bargaining unit 
agreements were also included to help the City 
respond to the competitive labor market and 
wage growth seen nationwide. If needed, 
adjustments will be made to cut back on 
expenses to manage City costs. 
The FY2023-24 budget continues the focus on 
the Council’s collective priorities including 
maintaining the Quality of Life for residents, 
Public Safety, Economic Development and 
addressing Homelessness.  It reflects our shared 
vision for the City of Oceanside—a thriving 
community that embraces fiscal responsibility, 
prioritizes community well-being, and champions 
environmental stewardship. It is a blueprint for 
progress, ensuring that we meet the challenges 
of today while building a resilient and prosperous 
future.
