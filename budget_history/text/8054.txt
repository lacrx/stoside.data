The annual operating budget document  
serves as the City’s monetary statement 
of program and service delivery for the 
next twelve months.  This brochure has 
been developed to show a snapshot of the 
general fund budget. 
 
The City of Oceanside has a balanced 
budget; revenue continues to show signs 
of growth.  Recent market data indicates 
that housing sales and values continue to 
improve with retail sales improving slightly 
over the past several years.  As tourism 
becomes a more important economic 
driver, transient occupancy tax (TOT) 
revenue is on the rise.  Developer-related 
fees also are remaining solid. 
 
The General Fund (GF) budget for FY 
2017-18 
includes 
a 
“rollover” 
of 
Maintenance & Operations (M&O) from 
the current year; however, a modest CPI 
of 
$400,000 
was 
included 
in 
the 
forecasted expenditure budget of $145.24 
million to better reflect known obligations 
such as multiple year contracts.  In 
Personnel 
Services 
the 
proposed 
expenditure allocation includes all known 
and negotiated personnel and benefit 
costs plus a place holder for the three 
labor 
associations 
that 
will 
be 
in 
negotiations in FY 2017-18. The rollover 
budget includes increases made to public 
safety in FY 2016-17 of $4.2 million which 
included 4 police officers, 1 police 
sergeant, 1 lifeguard sergeant, and 6 
permanent FF/Paramedics positions for 
the fifth ambulance and will continue to 
support various community services for 
gang intervention prevention, homeless 
outreach, and diversion programs for the 
City’s youth in challenged neighborhoods.
