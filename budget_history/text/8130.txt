SYNOPSIS
Staff submits the second quarter Financial Status Report and recommends that the City
Council accept the report and approve the budget adjustments.
BACKGROUND
The City Council adopted the FY 2021-22 budgets on June 2, 2021, with approved
General Fund revenues of $171.17 million and expenditures of $170.14 million. The City
adopted budget for all funds is $547.9 million in revenues and $546.79 million in
expenditures.
The report summarizes the preliminary General Fund major operating funds results as
of December 31, 2021, and is not intended to be inclusive of all transactions. It
compares budget and actual for revenues and expenditures. The ﬁnancial status of the
Water Utilities and Harbor enterprise funds are also included. The report is intended
only to provide Council and the public with a preliminary overview of the state of the
City's general ﬁscal condition as of December 31. 2021.
As of December 31, 2021, total General Fund revenues came in at $70 million and
represent 41 percent of total revenue budget. The revenue totals are 15 percent higher
than the second quarter of FY 2020-21. Total General Fund expenditures for the second
quarter amount to $81.6 million or 47 percent of the amended budget.

Property Taxes represent 44 percent of the total General Fund revenues and are primarily received in the
December/January and April/May time frames. Property tax revenues through December 31 are at 28 percent
of the amended budget. Compared to the prior year, property tax revenue increased 2 percent. The property
tax revenue increase is consistent with the projected increase in overall assessed values of properties in
Oceanside. Continued building development and increasing housing prices are expected to continue
throughout FY 2021-22.
Sales Taxes represent 14 percent of the total annual General Fund revenues. Revenues received through
December 31 are at 43 percent of the adopted budget and are 14 percent higher compared to the prior year.
The large increase in the current year is due to prior year decreased sales in the tourism sector and gasoline
stations in the prior year as a result of the shelter-in-place orders resulting from the COVID-19 pandemic. For
sales occurring in the third calendar quarter of 2021 (the most recent data available), key gains were seen in
service/gasoline stations and restaurants and hotels.
Transient Occupancy Tax (TOT) represent 4 percent of total General Fund revenues. The City collects 10
percent of the room rent charged by a hotel or vacation rental operator to tourists occupying a room within
the City for a period of less than 30 days. The City also collects a 1.5 percent assessment of the room rent
which is passed through to Visit Oceanside for marketing purposes. As of December 31, TOT is at 77 percent
of adopted budget. This is inclusive of the TOT revenue collected by Oceanside Beach Resort (OBR), totaling
$1M for the ﬁscal year as of December 31. Under the terms of the TOT Sharing Agreement with the developer
of OBR, the developer receives 100% of the TOT for the ﬁrst three years after the hotels open. Excluding the
OBR TOT revenue, the remaining TOT is 63 percent of adopted budget. This is up 36 percent compared to
prior year actuals due to decreased travel and tourism in the prior year as a result of the COVlD-19 pandemic.

ENTERPRISE FUNDS
Enterprise funds, also known as business-type activities, are used to account for the acquisition, operation
and maintenance of governmental facilities and services that are entirely or predominantly self-supporting
through user charges. The operations of Enterprise Funds are accounted for in such a manner as to show a
profit or loss similar to comparable private enterprise. The ﬁnancial status of operating funds for Water,
Sewer and Solid Waste Disposal are discussed below. Please note that Water and Sewer budgets consist of
multiple funds (operating, replacement and developer connection). Debt service is not included. Generally
accepted accounting principles (GAAP) call for ﬁscal analysis of Enterprise Funds to reflect a roll up of all funds
into one report.
WATER FUND REVENUES
Overall revenues for the Combined Water Funds are at 55 percent of the amended budget as of December 31
as shown in Table 4.
Water accounts receivables have increased during COVID-19. While the majority of customers are paying their
bills in full each month, there are businesses as well as residential customers that are struggling to pay their
bills. Staff is reaching out to the delinquent customers offering reasonable payment plans to help them
become current. In an effort to help delinquent customers, the City is currently offering an Oceanside
Ratepayer Relief Program of up to a $500 credit to assist Oceanside customers that are behind on their utility
bill. The program is available on a ﬁrst-come, ﬁrst-served basis to all single-family residential, multi-family,
commercial, irrigation, agriculture and recycled water customers that meet the program eligibility criteria and
requirements.
