Sphere of Influence under San Diego County zoning; the balance (**26,992 acres**,
Ch3 Table 3-1) is governed by the City of Oceanside Zoning Ordinance.

## 4. Observations relevant to financial projection

- **Residential is the dominant consumer of land** (36.0%, 9,716 ac); SFR alone is
  27.7% of the city — this drives the property-tax base more than any other category.
- **Roads/ROW is the single largest non-use category** at 4,258 ac (15.8%) —
  pairs with the Mobility Element curb-to-curb cross-sections if paved-surface
  area is needed.
- **Commercial + Industrial together occupy only 6.8%** (1,823 ac), yet these
  generate sales tax, TOT, and the bulk of non-residential property tax —
  look at Ch3's discussion of the SSCSP, CHID, Downtown, and Coastal Zone, which
  collectively cover ~80% of the City's operative commercial land.
- **Vacant inventory totals 1,015 ac (3.8%)**, already zoned. Residential-zoned
  vacant (SFR 426 + MFR 48 + Coastal PRD 44 = 518 ac) represents the clearest
  build-out revenue upside.
- **Agriculture at 2,861 ac (10.6%)** is large for a coastal city and is a rezoning
  candidate pool; note the GP designation retains 0.4 du/ac max density.
- **Ch3 vs BR2 discrepancy**: Ch3 total (26,992 ac) excludes the 20-ac SOI that
  BR2 (27,012 ac) includes — use Ch3 for City-limit projections, BR2 for
  Planning-Area totals.
